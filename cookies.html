1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit a website. We also use similar technologies such as local storage tokens. This policy explains what we use, why, and how you can control them, in compliance with the UK Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) and UK GDPR.

4. Third-party scheduler (Calendly)

The /demo page can embed Calendly for live booking. The Calendly iframe is not loaded automatically: a consent gate appears first, and Calendly only loads if you click "Load scheduler" or have previously granted marketing consent. When loaded, Calendly may set its own cookies on calendly.com (a separate origin we do not control).

Cookie / TokenProviderDomainPurposeRetention

6. How to Opt Out

  • Cookie consent banner: click the “Manage cookie preferences” button below to re-open the banner at any time and change your analytics preferences.
  • Browser settings: you can block or delete cookies in your browser settings. Note that blocking essential cookies will prevent you from logging in.
  • PostHog opt-out: visit PostHog opt-out page.
