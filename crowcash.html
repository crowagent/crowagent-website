Why CrowCash

Three reasons finance teams choose CrowCash

Cut DSO without hiring

Three chase cadences (polite / standard / firm) run automatically against your debtor book. Free up the bookkeeper's afternoon and reduce days sales outstanding by focusing effort where it counts.

Quote the law, not a guess

The statutory interest calculator applies the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998 - Bank of England base rate plus 8%, plus £40/£70/£100 statutory compensation per invoice. Defensible numbers in every chase letter.

Score every invoice

Payment-likelihood scoring per invoice, based on debtor history and ageing patterns. Focus the firm cadence on the invoices most at risk and the polite one on customers who always pay.

Platform features

Everything you need for accounts receivable

Chase sequence engine

Polite, standard, and firm cadences with editable templates. Triggered automatically by ageing buckets, and you can pause any cadence on a per-customer basis with one click.

Debtor ageing analytics

Real-time ageing buckets (current / 30 / 60 / 90+), DSO, collection rate, and dispute rate. Filter by customer, sales rep, or contract, and export to CSV for the management pack.

Statutory interest calculator

Per the Late Payment of Commercial Debts (Interest) Act 1998: Bank of England base rate + 8% interest, plus £40 (under £1,000), £70 (£1,000, £10,000), or £100 (over £10,000) statutory compensation. Cited in every demand letter.

Payment-likelihood scoring

Score every open invoice on a 0-100 scale based on debtor history, ageing, and dispute patterns. Trained on UK SME debtor behaviour, the model tells you which invoices need a phone call today.

CSV import + accounting integration roadmap

Import your debtor ledger from CSV today. Native Xero and QuickBooks two-way sync on the roadmap so the same invoice never gets chased twice.

Audit-ready chase log

Every email, letter, call note, and dispute is timestamped and linked to the invoice. Use the trail in court if a customer escalates, or hand it to your bookkeeper at year-end.
