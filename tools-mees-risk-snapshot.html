How the snapshot works

From postcode to defensible figure in three steps

Look up the EPC band

The snapshot reads the current EPC band straight from the MHCLG Open Data Communities register so the figure is grounded in the public record - not a self-declared estimate.

Apply the SI 2015/962 formula

Rateable-value penalty calculation per reg 39: 10% (<3-month breach) or 20% (≥3-month breach), constrained to the £5,000-£150,000 statutory band. No flat £30,000 myth.

Render a shareable summary

One-page output with the headline figure, the formula and statutory band that produced it, and citation lines for SI 2015/962 reg 39 and the MHCLG EPC register. Ready to share.

FAQ

Common questions

What is the maximum MEES fine?

Maximum fine is rateable-value-based, capped at £150,000 per SI 2015/962 reg 39.

Is Band C 2028 current law?

Band C 2028 is a proposed deadline, not yet confirmed law. MEES Band E (Energy Efficiency Regulations 2015) has applied since 2018 for new lettings and since April 2023 for continuing tenancies.
