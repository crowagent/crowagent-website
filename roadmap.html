METHOD

How we decide what to ship next

Every CrowAgent product traces back to a UK or EU regulation that already exists or has already passed Royal Assent. No speculative tools, no pivots to whatever VC trend is loudest this quarter. The roadmap above is built from four inputs, in this order:

  1. Regulatory deadlines. A statutory instrument with a date attached (MEES Band C 2028, CSRD waves under Omnibus I, PPN 014/21 supplier requirements) earns a slot before anything else.
  2. Customer pull. If 10+ existing customers ask for the same thing, it goes on the next quarter's plan. CrowESG is here because half our Core customers asked for it inside the first six weeks.
  3. Adjacency to a live product. CrowCash sits next to CrowAgent Core because the same finance lead at a property company writes both the chase email and the MEES brief. Adjacency reduces the cost of acquiring a new buyer.
  4. Where the edge sits. CrowAgent does not compete with horizontal ESG software giants on breadth; the edge is UK regulatory accuracy and time-to-output. Anything that does not play to that edge gets parked.
SCOPE

What we won't build

Honesty about scope is part of the deal. The list below is what people sometimes ask for that has been actively decided against, with the reason in each case so you can plan accordingly.

  • Generic ESG dashboards with no regulatory anchor. Plenty of vendors will sell you a colourful dashboard that maps to nothing. CrowAgent will not.
  • Carbon accounting platforms (Scope 1/2/3). The market has good players already (Watershed, Sweep, Persefoni). CrowESG will read your existing carbon data via API rather than re-implementing the engine.
  • EPR / WEEE / packaging tax tools. These are real regulations but adjacent to a buyer CrowAgent does not currently serve. Maybe in 2027.
  • White-label-only deployments. The CrowAgent brand is part of the audit trail. Agency tier supports co-branding for client-facing reports; the CrowAgent name is not stripped entirely.
  • Generative-AI compliance copilots that hallucinate citations. Every AI feature in the platform has a deterministic engine underneath. Narrative generation is the LAST step, never the SOURCE.
DRIVERS

Regulatory deadlines on the radar

The dates that shape the 2026-2028 roadmap. Each one is the source of truth for the corresponding CrowAgent product or planned addition.

  • 1 April 2026 (passed): MEES Band E minimum standard already applies to all let commercial property. Enforced by local weights-and-measures authorities under SI 2015/962.
  • 18 March 2026 (passed): Directive (EU) 2026/470 (Omnibus I) entered force, lifting CSRD thresholds to >1,000 employees AND >€450M turnover. CSRD Checker updated the same week.
  • 1 January 2027: CSRD Wave 2 reporting obligations begin under the revised Omnibus I phasing. Wave 2 covers large EU undertakings hitting both thresholds in the FY 2026 reporting year.
  • 1 April 2028 (proposed): MEES Band C minimum for commercial lettings, subject to legislative confirmation. CrowAgent Core penalty modelling already supports the proposed rule with a clear "proposed, not yet law" disclaimer in every report.
  • 2028 onwards: ISSB IFRS S1 & S2 mandated by an expanding set of jurisdictions; UK SDR application expected to widen from listed to private companies. CrowESG will track each jurisdiction's adoption gate.
