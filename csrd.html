Background

What is CSRD?

The Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive requires large and listed companies to disclose ESG performance under the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS).

Who is in scope
Large EU and non-EU companies with significant EU operations: more than 500 employees, or €40M+ turnover with 250+ employees, or listed on EU-regulated markets.
12 ESRS standards
Covers climate change, pollution, water, biodiversity, circular economy, own workforce, value chain workers, communities, consumers, and governance.
Mandatory assurance
CSRD reports require limited assurance from a registered auditor. First reports were due in 2025 for Phase 1 large companies (FY2024 data).
UK applicability
UK organisations with EU subsidiaries, operations, or listed securities may be in scope even post-Brexit. CSRD operates on an entity-by-entity basis.
Double materiality
Unlike TCFD, CSRD requires both financial materiality (impact on the company) and impact materiality (company's impact on the world).
Supply chain pressure
Even if CSRD doesn't apply directly, in-scope customers and buyers may require ESG data from their suppliers under ESRS S2 and G1 obligations.
