ICO data-controller registration

CrowAgent Ltd is registered as a data controller with the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) in accordance with the Data Protection (Charges and Information) Regulations 2018. Verifiable on the ICO public register by searching for “CrowAgent Ltd”.

Data we collect 02

  • Account data: email address, full name, and password hash (via Supabase Auth).
  • Property data: postcodes and address filters you submit for EPC lookups and MEES compliance checks.
  • Social Value data: procurement descriptions and submissions you enter for PPN 002 scoring.
  • Usage data: API call counts, feature usage events, timestamps, and subscription plan.
  • Billing data: Stripe customer ID and subscription status. We do not store raw card numbers.
  • Technical data: IP address, browser type, and error telemetry (collected by Sentry).
  • Analytics data: page views and feature interaction events (collected by PostHog with your consent).

How we use your data 03

  • To provide the CrowAgent service: authentication, EPC lookups, MEES checks, and Social Value scoring.
  • To process billing through Stripe.
  • To improve the product through aggregate analytics (consent-gated via PostHog).
  • To monitor and fix errors (via Sentry).

Legal basis for processing 04

  • Contract performance (Article 6(1)(b) UK GDPR): processing necessary to provide the CrowAgent service.
  • Legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR): error monitoring, fraud prevention, service security.
  • Consent (Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR): analytics tracking via PostHog.
  • Legal obligation (Article 6(1)(c) UK GDPR): financial records retention.

International data transfers 10

Where personal data is transferred outside the UK, we rely on one of the following lawful transfer mechanisms under Article 46 UK GDPR.

  • UK IDTA or EU SCCs + UK Addendum signed with each non-UK sub-processor.
  • Adequacy decisions issued by the UK Government where applicable (e.g. EEA, Switzerland).
  • Transfer Risk Assessments (TRAs) documented for every non-adequate destination.

Automated decision-making & AI 11

In accordance with Article 22 UK GDPR, we confirm the following.

No solely-automated significant decisions

CrowAgent does not make any decision about you that is based solely on automated processing and that produces legal effects (e.g. credit refusal, insurance underwriting, employment screening) or otherwise significantly affects you.

AI-assisted outputs are advisory

CrowMark uses Google Gemini (server-side) to draft social value narratives. CrowAgent Core uses deterministic rules-based engines for MEES and EPC analysis. All AI outputs are produced for review by a human bid team or compliance manager and must be edited and signed off by a human before submission.

No profiling for advertising or behavioural pricing

We do not profile users for marketing, do not run remarketing campaigns, and do not adjust pricing based on user attributes.

Right to human review

If you disagree with any AI-generated output produced inside the platform, you can edit it directly, contact dpo@crowagent.ai for a human review of any model decision, and request that a human re-runs the analysis.

Subject Access Request (DSAR) procedure 12

You can exercise your data-subject rights (Articles 15-21 UK GDPR) by following the steps below. The canonical landing inbox is privacy@crowagent.ai. dpo@crowagent.ai is monitored by the same DPO.

  1. Subject of request. Email privacy@crowagent.ai with the subject line “DSAR, [type of request]” (e.g. “DSAR, Access”, “DSAR, Erasure”, “DSAR, Portability”).
  2. What to include. The email address associated with your CrowAgent account, and a brief description of the data or action requested.
  3. Identity verification. Where we have reasonable doubt about identity, we may ask for proof (e.g. confirmation from the registered account email, or a copy of a government-issued ID for non-account holders) before disclosing personal data, in line with UK GDPR Article 12(6). We will not request more identity evidence than is necessary.
  4. Response SLA. We respond within one calendar month of receipt, in line with UK GDPR Article 12(3). The window may be extended by up to two further months for complex or numerous requests, in which case we will tell you within the first month and explain the reason.
  5. Fee & refusals. There is no fee for the first request. Per UK GDPR Article 12(5) we may charge a reasonable administrative fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, or refuse to act on them, with written reasons.
  6. If we cannot identify you from the information provided, we will say so and ask for clarification rather than refuse the request outright.

Right to lodge an ICO complaint

Independent of (and at any time during) this procedure, you have the right to complain to the UK ICO under UK GDPR Article 77. We do not retaliate against any individual who exercises this right.

What to expect after you submit a DSAR

You will receive an acknowledgement within 3 business days, an identity-verification step where applicable, and a substantive response within one calendar month. Erasure requests are reflected across all primary stores and backups within 30 days, with anonymised audit trails retained where legally required.

Google Gemini DPA (AI narrative generation) 13

CrowMark uses Google Gemini 1.5 Flash to draft social value narratives. We have signed Google’s Cloud Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and the Standard Contractual Clauses for international transfers.

  • Server-side key handling. The GEMINI_API_KEY is held exclusively as a Railway environment variable. It is never exposed to the browser and never embedded in any NEXT_PUBLIC_ variable.
  • No personal data passed to Gemini. Inputs are contract metadata (sector, value band, geography, framework selections) and the deterministic TOMs measure list. PII fields are stripped at the prompt boundary.
  • Hosting. The proxy that constructs each Gemini request runs on Railway (EU region). The Gemini endpoint is operated by Google in the US under SCCs + UK Addendum.
  • No training reuse. Per Google’s enterprise terms, prompts and completions submitted via our API key are not used to train or fine-tune Google’s foundation models.
  • Retention. Generated narratives are stored in our own Supabase database against the customer’s contract record (see Section 5). Google does not retain prompt or completion content beyond the abuse-monitoring window defined in the Cloud DPA.
  • Human review. All Gemini-generated narratives are advisory drafts reviewed and signed off by a human bid team before submission.

Sentry retention & error scrubbing 14

We use Sentry for client-side and server-side error monitoring. Sentry processing is bound by the following retention and minimisation rules.

  • Event retention, 90 days. Error events (stack traces, breadcrumbs, request metadata) are deleted from Sentry after 90 days.
  • Session replay retention, 30 days. When a session replay is associated with a captured error, the replay is deleted after 30 days.
  • Error scrubbing. Before transmission, the Sentry SDK scrubs default-sensitive keys (passwords, secret tokens, credit-card patterns, cookies, authorization headers) and applies our additional deny-list.
  • Input masking in replay. All HTML <input>, <textarea> and elements marked data-ph-mask are masked at capture time, so free-text user content never leaves the browser as cleartext.
  • Region. Sentry processes data in the US under SCCs + UK Addendum. EU residency for Sentry is on our backlog.
