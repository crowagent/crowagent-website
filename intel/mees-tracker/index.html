MEES Tracker — UK Commercial Property MEES Regulation Updates

Updated 3 May 2026 · CrowAgent Editorial · Living document

This is a living changelog of the Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards (MEES) for UK non-domestic property. The parent statutory instrument is SI 2015/962 — The Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015. We track every commencement date, amendment and government consultation that materially changes what a commercial landlord must do.

MEES applies to England and Wales. Scotland operates a separate but parallel regime under the Assessment of Energy Performance of Non-domestic Buildings (Scotland) Regulations 2016. Northern Ireland has no equivalent statutory MEES regime. Every entry below cites the underlying SI or amendment so you can verify it on legislation.gov.uk.

1 April 2028 PROPOSED

Band C deadline for commercial property — proposed, not enacted

The UK government has consulted on raising the minimum non-domestic MEES rating from Band E to Band C by 1 April 2028 (with an interim Band C requirement at lease initiation from 1 April 2027 in earlier consultation drafts). As of May 2026 this is a proposed regulatory target only — the implementing statutory instrument has not been laid before Parliament and the dates and bands could move as part of the wider Warm Homes Plan policy package.

  • Source consultation: Non-Domestic Private Rented Sector — Minimum Energy Efficiency Standards: EPC Band B by 2030 (originally consulted 2019/2020 by the then-BEIS, refreshed by DESNZ).
  • Subsequent communications have referenced an interim Band C step before any Band B target.
  • Until the amending SI is made, only Band E (see entries below) is enforceable as a matter of UK law.

Practical reading: treat Band C 2028 as a planning assumption with material commercial impact — but not as current law. Anyone who tells you the £30,000 flat fine "for failing Band C" is current law is wrong on both the band and the penalty formula.

1 April 2023

Band E becomes fully enforceable for all let commercial property

From 1 April 2023, a landlord may not continue to let a non-domestic property in England or Wales with an EPC rating of F or G unless a valid exemption is registered on the PRS Exemptions Register. The 2023 commencement closed the gap left in 2018 (which only applied at lease initiation) and brought the existing tenanted estate fully into scope.

  • Power: SI 2015/962 reg 27 (continued letting prohibition) commenced for non-domestic property on 1 April 2023.
  • Penalty regime: SI 2015/962 reg 39 — penalty calculated on rateable value, not a flat fine. Less than 3 months' breach = 10% of RV (min £5,000, max £50,000). 3 months or more = 20% of RV (min £10,000, max £150,000).
  • Publication penalty: an additional named publication on the PRS Exemptions Register that any prospective tenant or buyer can search.

1 April 2018

Band E required at lease initiation

The first hard MEES commencement. From 1 April 2018, a landlord could not grant a new lease (including a renewal or extension) on a non-domestic property in England or Wales with an EPC rating of F or G unless a valid exemption was registered. Existing leases granted before that date were not yet in scope — the regime was deliberately phased so the market had five years' notice before continued-letting was caught (2023, see above).

  • Power: SI 2015/962 reg 23 — restriction on granting a new tenancy of sub-standard property.
  • Exemptions register opened — landlords can register exemptions (high-cost, consent refused, devaluation, listed-building wall insulation) for 5-year terms.
  • Enforcement authority: the relevant local weights and measures authority (typically Trading Standards) for non-domestic property.

26 March 2015

SI 2015/962 made — Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) Regulations

The Energy Efficiency (Private Rented Property) (England and Wales) Regulations 2015 — the parent MEES instrument — were made on 26 March 2015 and laid before Parliament on the same day. They create the framework for both the domestic (Part 2) and non-domestic (Part 3) MEES regimes, the exemptions register, and the penalty notice powers.

  • Made under the Energy Act 2011, ss. 49 to 53.
  • Originally set the minimum standard at Band E, with phased commencement (lease initiation 2018, continued letting 2023).
  • Domestic and non-domestic regimes share the parent SI but have separate parts and separate enforcement bodies.
