1. What cookies are, in plain English

A cookie is a small text file a website saves to your browser. Some are essential (for example, to remember that you are signed in). Others are optional (for example, to count how many people visit a help page). We also use similar tools such as "local storage", which works the same way but lives inside your browser instead of being sent back to the server. This policy treats both the same.

UK regulatory basis

We follow the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations (PECR) for storage on your device, and the UK GDPR for any personal data those cookies carry. Read the rules at ico.org.uk/guide-to-pecr and ico.org.uk/guide-to-gdpr.

3. The three categories we use

We group cookies into three plain-English categories so you can choose what you are comfortable with.

Strictly necessary Always on

Keep you signed in, protect against bots, and remember your consent choice. These cannot be turned off because the site would not work without them.

Includes: ca_cookie_consent_v2, sb-access-token, sb-refresh-token, __cf_bm, cf_clearance, cf_chl_*, __cflb.

Analytics Optional

Help us understand which pages are used and where people get stuck. Hosted on PostHog EU cloud. Off by default.

Includes: ph_* (distinct ID, session ID, window/tab ID).

Marketing and scheduler Optional

Loads the Calendly demo scheduler on /demo. Off by default. Calendly is hosted in the US under Standard Contractual Clauses.

Includes: _calendly_session, _cfuvid.

Essential cookies only by default

On your first visit, only the strictly necessary cookies are placed. Analytics and marketing stay off until you opt in. If you reject the banner, nothing optional is set.

Analytics requires consent

PostHog product analytics is only loaded after you tick "Analytics" in the banner. If you change your mind, "Manage cookie preferences" re-opens the dialog and the analytics tag is unloaded on the next page view.

PECR consent requirement

Under PECR reg 6 we cannot place any non-essential cookie on your device without your prior, freely-given, specific, and informed consent. The banner gives you a single click to accept, reject, or pick category-by-category.

Some cookies are set by trusted providers we use to run the site. Their privacy policies and opt-out pages are below.
