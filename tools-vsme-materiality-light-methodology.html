1. Why VSME exists

The locked wording: EFRAG VSME (2024) is the SME-friendly path. When CSRD was originally scoped, EFRAG drafted a separate VSME standard for entities not in scope. The 2024 release is the version we follow. It is a voluntary standard but is becoming the de-facto reporting form when supply-chain customers, lenders, or insurers ask SMEs for sustainability data without mandating full ESRS compliance.

2. The six steps in the screen

  1. Business model: products, services, geographies, and the dependencies on natural and social capital that drive each.
  2. Value chain: upstream and downstream dependencies, with the implications for indirect impacts and risks.
  3. Stakeholders: who is affected by the business and who can affect it. Light input rather than full stakeholder engagement, intentionally appropriate to SME resourcing.
  4. Impact identification: where the business has positive or negative impacts on people and planet.
  5. Financial-effects identification: where sustainability matters create financial risks or opportunities for the business.
  6. Materiality scoring: traffic-light scoring on each topic, plotted on the standard impact-vs-financial matrix.

3. Double materiality, lite

VSME endorses the same double-materiality concept that ESRS uses but applies it at a lower depth appropriate to SMEs. Both axes (impact materiality and financial materiality) are assessed, but with simpler scoring scales and lighter stakeholder evidence requirements than the full ESRS approach.

The Light tool deliberately does not collect long-form stakeholder survey data. The full CrowESG platform (Coming Q3 2026) handles that.

4. The output matrix

Output is a printable two-by-two matrix with topics plotted by impact and financial materiality scores. Each topic appears in one of four quadrants: high impact / high financial (priority disclosures), high impact / low financial, low impact / high financial, or low / low (drop from current scope, monitor). The matrix is exportable for board packs, supply-chain customer requests, or lender ESG questionnaires.

5. Topics covered

The screen presents the standard VSME topic list across environmental, social, and governance pillars: climate change, pollution, water and marine, biodiversity, circular economy, own workforce, value-chain workers, affected communities, consumers and end-users, business conduct. Users can add bespoke topics where the standard list does not capture sector-specific issues.

6. What VSME Light does not do

It does not produce a full ESRS-compliant report. It does not satisfy CSRD reporting obligations for in-scope companies. It does not run formal stakeholder engagement, double-materiality with audit assurance, or cross-framework metric mapping. Those capabilities live in the full CrowESG platform (Coming Q3 2026, on the waitlist).

7. Use cases that fit

  • SMEs receiving supply-chain ESG questionnaires from larger customers.
  • SME boards setting the year-one materiality view ahead of an investor or lender request.
  • Voluntary publication on a corporate website to signal sustainability maturity.
  • Pre-work for entities that may move into CSRD scope in 24–36 months.
