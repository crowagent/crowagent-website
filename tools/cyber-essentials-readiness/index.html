Methodology & sources

Why you can trust this verdict

  • Five control families come from the NCSC / IASME Cyber Essentials Requirements for IT Infrastructure v3.3 (Danzell, in force 28 April 2026).
  • Pass-thresholds: 5/5 = likely to pass, 2-4/5 = at risk, 0-1/5 = not ready.
  • IASME enforces 'any single fail = certification fail', the verdict reflects this.
  • The assessor questionnaire has 60+ specific controls; this 5-question screen is a pre-screen, not a guarantee of certification.
