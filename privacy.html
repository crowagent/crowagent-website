12. Automated Decision-Making and Profiling

In accordance with Article 22 UK GDPR, we confirm:

  • No solely-automated decisions with legal or similarly significant effects. CrowAgent does not make any decision about you that is based solely on automated processing and that produces legal effects (e.g. credit refusal, insurance underwriting, employment screening) or otherwise significantly affects you.
  • AI-assisted outputs are advisory only. CrowMark uses Google Gemini (server-side) to draft social value narratives and CrowAgent Core uses deterministic rules-based engines for MEES and EPC analysis. All AI outputs are produced for review by the customer’s human bid team or compliance manager and must be edited and signed off by a human before submission. The customer remains the controller of the final output.
  • No profiling for advertising or behavioural pricing. We do not profile users for marketing, do not run remarketing campaigns, and do not adjust pricing based on user attributes.
  • Right to human review. If you disagree with any AI-generated output produced inside the platform, you can edit it directly, contact dpo@crowagent.ai for a human review of any model decision, and request that a human re-runs the analysis.

13. Subject Access & Other Data-Subject Requests (DSAR procedure)

You can exercise the following data-subject rights through this procedure:

  • Subject access (Article 15) — a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Rectification (Article 16) — correction of inaccurate data.
  • Erasure (Article 17, “right to be forgotten”) — deletion of your personal data subject to retention exceptions.
  • Restriction of processing (Article 18).
  • Data portability (Article 20) — a machine-readable export of your account data.
  • Objection (Article 21) — including profiling.

Procedure to make any of the above requests:

  1. Subject of request. Email privacy@crowagent.ai (or dpo@crowagent.ai — both inboxes are monitored by the same DPO) with the subject line “DSAR — [type of request]” (e.g. “DSAR — Access”, “DSAR — Erasure”, “DSAR — Portability”).
  2. What to include. The email address associated with your CrowAgent account and a brief description of the data or action requested.
  3. Identity verification. Where we have reasonable doubt about identity, we may ask for proof (e.g. confirmation from the registered account email, or a copy of a government-issued ID for non-account holders) before disclosing personal data, in line with UK GDPR Article 12(6). We will not request more identity evidence than is necessary.
  4. Response SLA. We respond within one calendar month of receipt, in line with UK GDPR Article 12(3). The response window may be extended by up to two further months for complex or numerous requests, in which case we will tell you within the first month and explain the reason.
  5. Fee & refusals. There is no fee for the first request. Per UK GDPR Article 12(5) we may charge a reasonable administrative fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests, or refuse to act on them, with written reasons.
  6. If we cannot identify you from the information provided, we will say so and ask for clarification rather than refuse the request outright.

Right to lodge an ICO complaint. Independent of (and at any time during) this procedure, you have the right to complain to the UK Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) under UK GDPR Article 77. ICO contact details are in Section 8 (and consolidated in Section 17). We do not retaliate against any individual who exercises this right.

15. Google Gemini Data Processing Agreement (AI narrative generation)

CrowMark uses Google Gemini 1.5 Flash to draft social value narratives. We have signed Google’s Cloud Data Processing Addendum (DPA) and the Standard Contractual Clauses for international transfers. The processing arrangement is as follows:

  • Server-side only key handling. The GEMINI_API_KEY credential is held exclusively as a Railway environment variable on our backend. It is never exposed to the browser, never embedded in any NEXT_PUBLIC_ variable, and never present in any frontend bundle.
  • No personal data passed to Gemini. Inputs sent to Gemini consist of contract metadata (sector, value band, geography, framework selections) and the deterministic TOMs measure list. Identifiers such as user email, organisation name, free-text PII fields, IP addresses, and Stripe customer IDs are stripped or never collected at the prompt boundary.
  • Hosting. The proxy that constructs and signs each Gemini request runs on Railway (EU region). The Gemini endpoint itself is operated by Google in the US under the Standard Contractual Clauses + UK Addendum (see Section 11).
  • No training reuse. Per Google’s enterprise terms applicable to our DPA, prompts and completions submitted via our API key are not used to train, fine-tune, or improve Google’s foundation models.
  • Retention. Generated narratives are stored in our own Supabase database against the customer’s contract record (subject to the retention rules in Section 5). Google does not retain prompt or completion content beyond the abuse-monitoring window defined in the Cloud DPA.
  • Human review. All Gemini-generated narratives are advisory drafts and are reviewed and signed off by the customer’s human bid team before submission (see Section 12).

16. Sentry retention and error scrubbing

We use Sentry for client-side and server-side error monitoring. Sentry processing is bound by the following retention and minimisation rules:

  • Event retention: 90 days. Error events (stack traces, breadcrumbs, request metadata) are deleted from Sentry after 90 days.
  • Session replay retention: 30 days. When a session replay is associated with a captured error, the replay is deleted after 30 days.
  • Error scrubbing. Before transmission, the Sentry SDK scrubs default-sensitive keys (passwords, secret/auth tokens, credit-card patterns, cookies, authorization headers) and applies our additional deny-list for application-specific identifiers.
  • Input masking in replay. All HTML <input>, <textarea> and elements marked data-ph-mask are masked at capture time, so free-text user content never leaves the browser as cleartext.
  • Region. Sentry processes data in the US under Standard Contractual Clauses + UK Addendum (see Section 11). EU residency for Sentry is on our backlog.
