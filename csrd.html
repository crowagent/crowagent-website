Why CSRD Checker

Three reasons sustainability teams use CSRD Checker

Post-Omnibus I clarity

Updated for Directive (EU) 2026/470 in force 18 March 2026. Both thresholds required: more than 1,000 employees AND more than €450M turnover. The old 250-employee / €40M floor is superseded.

Three-layer engine

Layer 1 mandatory scope, Layer 2 forward risk (cross both thresholds within 2 reporting years), and Layer 3 value-chain exposure for groups with in-scope parents or customers. The same engine assurers expect.

No signup required

Run the test, get the verdict, share the result. No account, no email gate, no credit card. The full citation to Directive (EU) 2026/470 is in every output so you can defend the answer to a board or a regulator.

Tool features

Everything you need to test CSRD scope

Layer 1: mandatory scope

Threshold test against the post-Omnibus I criteria: more than 1,000 employees AND more than €450M turnover, both required. Returns a clear In-scope / Out-of-scope verdict with the source citation.

Layer 2: forward risk

Identifies groups likely to cross both thresholds within two reporting years. Flags forward-risk status so you can start gap analysis before the mandatory date arrives, not the week before.

Layer 3: value-chain exposure

Highlights when an out-of-scope SME nonetheless faces de-facto reporting through an in-scope parent or major customer. The exposure point most threshold tools miss.

EU member state transposition lookup

Each member state transposes Omnibus I on its own timeline. The checker references the latest published transposition status so a UK group with EU subsidiaries gets the right effective date.

PDF export

Download a one-page result with your inputs, the verdict, and the regulatory citation. Suitable for sharing with the audit committee, the board, or your assurer as a first-pass scoping document.
